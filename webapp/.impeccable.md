# Design Context — Office of Accountability

## Target Audience
General public, journalists, activists, researchers, developers. Not a niche tool — anyone should be able to read and navigate the investigations.

## Use Cases
- Read investigation narratives (long-form articles with data)
- Explore interactive graphs and timelines
- Verify claims against sources
- Understand the engine's methodology

## Brand Personality / Tone
**Editorial newspaper aesthetic with bold color accents.** Dark background, serif mastheads, structured chapters. The feel is a serious investigative publication — not a dashboard, not a SaaS product. Think: long-form journalism meets data visualization.

Bold accents in color used sparingly for emphasis — case colors (purple, red, emerald, sky, amber, stone, yellow) already exist per investigation. These should feel intentional, not decorative.

## What Should Someone Remember
Three things, in order:
1. **The results** — real investigations with real findings already published
2. **The system** — an engine that works, in active development
3. **The accountability potential** — what this becomes when the engine is open

## Aesthetic Direction
- **Editorial/newspaper** as the foundation — serif typography for headings, clean hierarchy
- **Dark mode** with zinc/neutral tones — not the AI-slop cyan-on-dark; warm zinc tints
- **Bold color accents** per case — used for dots, lines, links, not backgrounds
- **Scroll-reveal animations** — chapters appear as you scroll, building narrative momentum
- **Dot-grid pattern** background — subtle texture, not noise
- **Asymmetric rhythm** — chapter connectors (colored lines + dots) break the monotony
- **Data as decoration** — numbers and stats ARE the visual interest, not icons or illustrations

## Anti-Patterns (DO NOT)
- No glassmorphism, no glow borders, no gradient text
- No card-heavy layouts — the narrative is the layout
- No SaaS dashboard aesthetics
- No emojis, no rounded-rectangle cards with drop shadows
- No "AI-made" fingerprints (purple-to-blue gradients, neon accents)
