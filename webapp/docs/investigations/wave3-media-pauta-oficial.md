## 1. PAUTA OFICIAL (State Advertising) - Distribution Map

### 1.1 National Level: Fernandez Government (2019-2023)

- **Total spend 2023:** ~$40 billion ARS (~$120 billion at Sept 2024 values)
- **Media recipients:** Expanded from 1,400 (Macri era) to 3,800 in 2023
- **Top 3 groups by spend (2023):**
  - Grupo Clarin: $4,100M ARS (~USD 11M) - largest single recipient
  - Grupo Indalo (Cristobal Lopez)
  - Grupo Octubre (Victor Santa Maria)
- **YouTube** was the single largest individual recipient: $1,079M ARS
- **Telefe Buenos Aires:** $907M ARS
- **Infobae:** $876M ARS
- **TV** took 26% of total; web portals (24%) + social media (6%) = 30% combined
- **Clarin received $1 of every $8** in official advertising
- **Community/cooperative media:** only 1.7% of total budget
- **76% of budget** went to Buenos Aires-based companies

### 1.2 National Level: Milei Government (2024-present)

- **"Pauta cero" policy:** Decree 89/2023 suspended official advertising
- **Central administration spending Jan-Sept 2024:** only 1.2% of 2023 levels
- **Mandatory legal announcements only:** $96M ARS through July 2024
- **Suspension extended for 2025** via Resolution 7147/2024

**BUT: The "paraestatal" channel via state companies:**
- **YPF 2024:** $97,110M ARS in advertising - **39.9% increase** vs. last year of Fernandez
- YPF **refuses to disclose** which media outlets receive funds (defying Agencia de Acceso a la Informacion Publica rulings)

- **Banco Nacion:** advertising spend up 40% in real terms
- **Aerolineas Argentinas:** down 82.5% (from USD 3.4M to USD 618K)

**Key finding:** Milei's "pauta cero" is functionally a rebrand. YPF serves as the opaque channel for directing advertising money to aligned media, with no public disclosure of recipients.

## 2. LEY DE MEDIOS (Media Law) - Regulatory History

### 2.1 Law 26,522 (2009) - Ley de Servicios de Comunicacion Audiovisual

- Enacted October 10, 2009 under Cristina Fernandez de Kirchner
- Conceived communication as a **human right**
- **Key restrictions:**
  - Maximum 24 cable licenses per operator
  - Cross-ownership limits (can't hold cable TV + radio/open TV simultaneously)
  - Licenses are non-transferable
  - One-year deadline to divest excess licenses
- **Grupo Clarin** challenged articles 41, 45, 48, and 161 as unconstitutional
- Obtained precautionary injunction exempting it from compliance
- **"7D" (Dec 7, 2012):** Government deadline for voluntary divestment plans - blocked by court ruling on Dec 6
- **Supreme Court (Oct 29, 2013):** Ruled articles 41 and 45 **constitutional** (6 votes in favor)
- Despite ruling, Clarin never fully divested

### 2.2 Macri Decree 267/2015 - Gutting the Law

- **DNU (Decreto de Necesidad y Urgencia) 267/2015** published Dec 31, 2015
- **Merged AFSCA + AFTIC** into new entity **ENACOM**
- **Key changes:**
  - Removed 24-city cap on cable operators
  - Allowed license transfers (previously non-transferable)
  - Eliminated cross-ownership restrictions between pay TV and radio/open TV
  - Extended all existing licenses by 10 years
  - Archived all pending divestment plans - **no obligation to sell signals**
- **Ratified by Congress** April 2016
- **Effect:** Legalized Clarin's existing concentration and enabled further expansion

### 2.3 Milei Era - ENACOM Intervention and Deregulation (2024-present)

- **Decree 89/2024:** ENACOM intervened for 180 days (renewable); **Martin Ozores** as interventor
- **~300 ENACOM employees fired** by March 2024
- **Deregulated telecommunications pricing:** State no longer sets internet, cable, or mobile prices
- **Derogated must-carry obligations:** Cable operators no longer required to include national/provincial news channels (C5N, La Nacion+, Cronica TV, IP at risk)
- **Decree 512/2025:** New organizational structure, positions suppressed
- **Closed regional delegations**
- Working on a unified Law of Communications to merge audiovisual and telecom regulation

## 3. TELECOM-CLARIN MONOPOLY - Telecommunications Concentration

### 3.1 The Acquisition

- **Telecom Argentina** (controlled by Grupo Clarin + Fintech fund) purchased **Telefonica Argentina (Movistar)** for **USD 1,245 million**
- Creates a near-duopoly with only **AMX (Claro)** as competitor

### 3.2 Market Share Post-Merger

| Service | Combined Telecom+Telefonica share |
|---------|----------------------------------|
| Fixed telephone | 79% |
| Mobile telephone | 61% (58% Telecom + 42% Claro - only 2 players left) |
| Broadband internet | 48% |
| Pay TV | 42% |

- In Buenos Aires, Neuquen, Mendoza: combined shares reach **up to 87%** in some zones
- Spectrum accumulation exceeds ENACOM's **140 MHz limit** by up to 130 MHz in multiple locations
- Only operator with national capacity for **quadruple play** bundles

### 3.3 Regulatory Response

- **CNDC (Comision Nacional de Defensa de la Competencia)** issued formal **Objection Report**
- Government demanded Telecom propose alternatives
- **Telecentro** filed complaint with CNDC
- **ENACOM** warned about monopolistic concentration risk
- As of March 2026, timeline for approval remains **suspended**

## 4. DIGITAL ADVERTISING LANDSCAPE

### 4.1 Market Structure

- **Digital advertising** represented **39% of total ad budgets** in 2024 (up from 26% in 2015)
- **Television** held 40% (up from 34% in 2015)
- **2025 projections:** Digital +79% growth, TV +60%, print +52%, radio +43%
- **Digital revenue for Argentine media** grew 132.2% since 2022
- **Globally:** Google, Meta, and Amazon capture **60%+** of digital advertising revenue

### 4.2 Fundacion Faro - Dark Money in Digital Political Advertising

- **Think tank** led by **Agustin Laje**, launched Nov 13, 2024 with Milei in attendance
- **3 registered brands:** Fundacion Faro Argentina, Academia Libres del Mundo, **Ratio Oficial**
- **Ratio Oficial** spent **$1,079 million ARS (~USD 821,000)** on Meta political ads (March 2025-March 2026) - over 15,000 posts
- **Second-largest political advertiser on Meta** in Argentina (behind Cabinet Secretariat)
- **Has not filed financial statements** with IGJ (Inspeccion General de Justicia)
- **Will not disclose** origin of funds for advertising
- **Purpose:** "cultural battle from social networks" for La Libertad Avanza

## 5. PRESS FREEDOM STATUS

### 5.1 RSF World Press Freedom Index

- Argentina **dropped 47 positions** in two years under Milei, to **rank 87 of 180**
- **Causes cited by RSF:**
  - Milei stigmatizes journalists
  - Dismantled public media
  - Uses state advertising as political weapon
- **Violence at protests:** Security forces systematically targeting journalists covering demonstrations

### 5.2 Attacks on Journalists (FOPEA Data)

- **2024:** 179 attacks documented - **53% increase** over 2023, **103% increase** over 2022
- **52.5% of attacks** caused by political actors
- **Milei personally** mentioned in 56 cases (stigmatizing discourse, insults)
- **240,000 pejorative mentions** of journalism by Milei and allied accounts in 2024 - **2,000% increase** over 2023
- **Attack types:** Stigmatizing discourse (45.25%), physical attacks (25.14%), access restrictions (11.73%)
- **2025:** Attacks grew **60%+ more**
- **Resolution 1319/25:** Restricted journalist access to Casa Rosada with discretionary accreditation system, dress code, and circulation limits

### 5.3 Government Censorship and Judicial Harassment

- Government obtained **judicial order to censor** publication of recordings compromising officials in corruption
- **Two journalists** criminally charged; government requested **home raids** and demanded they **reveal sources**
- International Federation of Journalists condemned actions

### 5.4 SIDE (Intelligence Agency) Surveillance

- **Plan de Inteligencia Nacional (PIN)** targets those who "manipulate public opinion" or "erode confidence" in officials
- Vague language covers journalists, academics, economists, opposition figures
- **Budget:** 25,250M ARS extra allocated; total over 80,000M ARS executed + DNU 656/24 granting additional 100,000M ARS without legislative oversight
- **Hugo Alconada Mon** (investigative journalist) reported attempted hacking of WhatsApp and X accounts
- **CELS** (civil rights org) warned the intelligence plan broadens secrecy and enables domestic spying

## 6. SYNTHESIS - How the System Works

### The Pauta-Media-Politics Triangle

```
                    GOVERNMENT
                   /          \
          pauta $$ /            \ regulation
                 /              \
           MEDIA ──────────────── CORPORATIONS
              favorable coverage    ownership overlap
```

**Every Argentine government** has used pauta oficial as reward/punishment:
- **Kirchnerism (2003-2015):** Expanded recipients to 3,800 but concentrated on aligned groups (Indalo, Octubre); weaponized Ley de Medios against Clarin
- **Macri (2015-2019):** Gutted Ley de Medios via DNU 267, legalized Clarin's concentration, shifted advertising to aligned outlets
- **Milei (2024-present):** Declared "pauta cero" but funnels money through YPF ($97B ARS in 2024, opaque distribution); deregulates ENACOM; Fundacion Faro spends $1B+ ARS in dark money on Meta political ads; SIDE surveillance plan targets journalists
