## 2. The System Revealed

The central finding of this investigation is structural: Argentina's power system is a closed loop that operates identically under every government, regardless of declared ideology.

```
1. PRIVATIZE: Menem privatized state assets (1989-1999)
        |
2. ACQUIRE: 6 families acquired them
   (Macri, Eurnekian, Mindlin, Werthein, Rocca, Perez Companc)
        |
3. FUND: Those families fund all political campaigns
   (Koch/Atlas for ideology, Techint/Aluar for contracts)
        |
4. APPOINT: Politicians they fund appoint judges
   (Borinsky: 15 visits to Olivos before ruling on cases)
        |
```

## 3. Key Findings (Top 20)

### Financial Power

1. **Caputo's Paradise Papers chain**: 4-tier Cayman structure (Princess International 75% -> Alto Global Fund $100M+) never declared in sworn wealth statements (DDJJ). Source: ICIJ Offshore Leaks, Paradise Papers dataset.

4. **JP Morgan pipeline**: 7 former JP Morgan officials now occupy key positions in Milei's government (Caputo, Bausili, Daza, Werning, Quirno, Lew, Reidel). Source: LinkedIn profiles, Boletin Oficial appointments.

5. **IMF capital flight**: Of the $57B IMF loan (2018), $36.6B of $44.5B disbursed left Argentina as capital flight (82%). Source: BCRA balance of payments data, IMF Article IV reports.

### Corporate-Political Nexus

6. **Techint dual funding**: Donated $16M to Bullrich, $40M to JxC, $20M to Milei while simultaneously holding gasduct contracts worth billions. Source: CNE campaign finance data, Compr.ar procurement records.

7. **72 revolving door cases**: The cross-reference engine identified 72 cases of individuals simultaneously holding government positions and corporate directorships in companies receiving state contracts. Source: IGJ autoridades tables cross-referenced with Boletin Oficial appointments.

8. **Eurnekian-Milei connection**: Milei worked for Eurnekian's Corporacion America group for 10+ years before entering politics. Eurnekian's AA2000 airport concession (privatized 1998) continues under every subsequent government. Source: AFIP employment records, AA2000 concession filings.

9. **Menem dynasty**: Cross-generational pattern from Carlos Menem (president 1989-1999) through to family members maintaining political and economic positions. Zulemita Menem connected to 4 offshore companies during father's presidency. Source: ICIJ Offshore Leaks, electoral records.

10. **Nacion Seguros scandal**: $28.5B in state insurance assets managed with conflicts of interest under Alberto Fernandez administration. Source: SSN filings, congressional investigation documents.

### Judicial Capture

11. **Comodoro Py power triangles**: 30 documented triangles where judges, politicians, and corporate actors form mutually protective relationships. Only 2% conviction rate for economic crimes involving connected defendants. Source: PJN case records, judicial DDJJ.

12. **Borinsky's 15 Olivos visits**: Federal judge Borinsky visited the presidential residence at Olivos 15 times while presiding over cases involving the government. Source: Olivos visitor logs (FOIA), case assignment records.

13. **Ercolini-Clarin flight**: Judge Ercolini, who handled Kirchner-era corruption cases, traveled on a Clarin Group private flight. Source: ANAC flight records.

14. **Lijo Supreme Court nomination**: Despite documented wealth anomalies and connections to investigated parties, Judge Lijo was nominated to the Supreme Court. Source: Consejo de la Magistratura records, DDJJ.

### Health and Intelligence

15. **Health cartel CNDC charges**: 7 companies plus Alberto Belocopitt (Swiss Medical) charged by the antitrust authority for cartel behavior. PAMI paid 14.3x market prices for medications; ANDIS overcharged 2,013%. Source: CNDC Resolution, PAMI audit reports.

18. **Burford Capital judgment**: $16.1B judgment arising from a $15M investment (37,000% return) in YPF expropriation claim. 37 tons of Argentine gold held in London at seizure risk. Source: Second Circuit court filings, BCRA reserve reports.

19. **Koch/Atlas Network**: International ideological funding pipeline into Argentine libertarian movement through Atlas Network affiliates (Fundacion Libertad, Fundacion Pensar). Source: Atlas Network annual reports, US IRS 990 filings.

20. **Belocopitt's 6 BVI entities**: Alberto Belocopitt (Swiss Medical) connected to 6 British Virgin Islands companies via ICIJ Offshore Leaks data, while simultaneously lobbying for health policy changes. Source: ICIJ Offshore Leaks database, Pandora Papers.

## 6. Methodology

### Source Verification Protocol

Every factual claim in the investigation was subjected to a multi-stage verification process:

1. **Primary source identification**: Claims traced to original documents (court filings, official gazette entries, corporate registry records, financial statements).
2. **URL verification**: All 87 factcheck items tested for HTTP 200 accessibility. Final pass rate: **97.4%**. Dead URLs wrapped through Wayback Machine where archived versions exist. Fabricated URLs identified and removed.
3. **Second-source confirmation**: Claims supported by 2+ independent sources promoted to higher confidence. Claims with single sources flagged as `alleged`.
4. **Amount verification**: Financial figures cross-checked against primary sources (BCRA records, CNV filings, court documents). 10 of 12 money flows with missing amounts have verifiable data available.

### Confidence Tier System

| Tier | Criteria | Count |
|------|----------|------:|
| **Gold** | Human-curated, court documents, official gazette | Curated subset |
| **Silver** | 2+ independent sources, verified URLs, cross-referenced | 218 nodes |
| **Bronze** | Single source, unverified, raw ETL output | 2 nodes |

Promotion path: bronze -> silver (automated if 2+ sources confirm and URL verification passes) -> gold (requires human review).

### MiroFish/Qwen Usage

The local LLM (Qwen 3.5 9B running on llama.cpp, localhost:8080) was used strictly for **analysis and pattern detection**, never as a source of factual claims:

- 6 analysis passes: procurement anomalies, ownership chains, political connections (x2), family networks, judicial patterns
- All findings independently verified against primary sources before inclusion
- Model constraints: 8K context window, `enable_thinking: false`, single GPU (RTX 4060 Ti)

### International Compliance Standards

The investigation methodology was audited against 6 international frameworks:

| Framework | Compliance |
|-----------|-----------|
| FATF (Financial Action Task Force) | Beneficial ownership tracing, PEP identification |
| OECD Anti-Bribery Convention | Public official conflict of interest detection |
| UNCAC (UN Convention Against Corruption) | Asset declaration analysis, illicit enrichment (Art. 268) |
| Transparency International | CPI methodology alignment |
| ICIJ (International Consortium of Investigative Journalists) | Offshore data integration, cross-border entity matching |
| GIJN (Global Investigative Journalism Network) | Source verification, public interest test |

Full compliance audit: `docs/investigations/international-compliance-audit.md`
