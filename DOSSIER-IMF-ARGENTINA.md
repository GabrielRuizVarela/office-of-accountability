## 1. IMF $57B Loan (2018) — Timeline and Accountability Gaps

### Timeline

| Date | Event |
|------|-------|
| Dec 2015 | Mauricio Macri takes office; lifts capital controls ("cepo") |
| 2016-2017 | Argentina issues massive external debt (~$100B in bonds) |
| Apr 2018 | Currency crisis begins; peso depreciates sharply |
| Jun 2018 | IMF approves 36-month Stand-By Arrangement (SBA) for $50B |
| Oct 2018 | SBA increased to $57B — largest loan in IMF history, 21st to Argentina |
| 2018-2019 | IMF disburses $44.5B of the $57B commitment |
| Aug 2019 | Macri loses presidential primaries; program goes off rails |
| Dec 2019 | Alberto Fernandez takes office; suspends remaining IMF tranches |
| Dec 2021 | IMF Ex-Post Evaluation (EPE) published: "did not deliver on its objectives" |
| Mar 2022 | New Extended Fund Facility (EFF) agreed under Fernandez to refinance Macri-era debt |
| Jan 2025 | IMF EPE of 2022 EFF: also "did not achieve its objectives" |

### Accountability Gaps

- **No capital controls required:** The IMF acknowledged it should have pushed Argentina to implement capital controls. Without them, loan proceeds flowed directly out as capital flight.
- **Strategy "too fragile":** The IMF's own evaluation called the strategy "too fragile to deal with such a high-risk scenario."
- **Concentrated beneficiaries:** The top 100 individuals and firms accounted for $24.7B (28.7%) of $86B in capital flight. The top 1% of companies were behind $41.1B (47.8%).
- **No ex-ante accountability:** No mechanism existed to track whether IMF disbursements were used for their stated purpose (stabilizing the peso and restoring confidence).
- **Reputational damage acknowledged:** The IMF admitted the loan created "substantial financial and reputational risks for the Fund."

## 2. Capital Flight Quantification

### Macri Era (2015-2019) — BCRA Official Data

| Metric | Amount | Source |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Total capital flight (FAE) 2015-2019 | USD $86.2B | BCRA official report |
| Top 100 entities' share | USD $24.7B (28.7%) | BCRA report |
| Top 1% of companies' share | USD $41.1B (47.8%) | BCRA report |
| IMF disbursed | USD $44.5B | IMF records |
| Capital flight during IMF program | USD $36.6B | Multiple sources |

### Key Finding: IMF Money Financed Capital Flight

Of the $44.5B the IMF disbursed, approximately $36.6B left through capital flight — an 82% leakage rate. The BCRA concluded that "the set of economic policies applied since December 2015 facilitated capital flight of more than $86 billion."

During the capital inflow phase (Dec 2015 - early 2018), approximately 8 out of every 10 dollars that entered Argentina from abroad originated in debt placements and speculative capital.

### Named Entities ("Los 100 de Macri")

The BCRA report identified 100 top capital flight agents but did not name them. Investigative media subsequently identified key names:
- **Grupo Clarin** (Hector Magnetto)
- **Techint** (Paolo Rocca)
- **Arcor** (Luis Pagani)
- **Pampa Energia** (Marcelo Mindlin)
- **Aceitera General Deheza**
- **Nicolas Caputo** (Macri's personal friend and business associate)
- **Cristiano Ratazzi**

### Historical Cumulative Capital Flight

- 1976-2015: approximately USD $321B extracted from the national financial system
- 2016-2019: additional USD $100B in wealth externalization

### BCRA Reserves Evolution

| Date | Gross Reserves |
|------|---------------|
| Apr 2023 | USD $38.7B |
| Mar 2024 | USD $27.8B |
| Mar 2025 | USD $25.0B (pre-IMF disbursement) |

The reserves trajectory reveals structural dependency: reserves only recovered to 2023 levels after the April 2025 IMF injection of $12B.

## 3. How IMF Money Connected to Domestic Networks

### The Mechanism

```
IMF Disbursement → BCRA Reserves → FX Market Intervention → Dollar Sales to Private Sector
→ Capital Flight (FAE) → Offshore Accounts (US, Switzerland, Uruguay, etc.)
```

1. **Debt issuance phase (2016-2018):** Macri's government issued ~$100B in bonds. 8 of every 10 dollars entering came from debt placements.
2. **IMF phase (Jun 2018-2019):** IMF disbursed $44.5B to defend the peso. Dollars sold on the FX market were purchased by the same concentrated economic groups.
3. **Capital flight phase:** $36.6B left the country during the IMF program. The top 100 buyers were a who's-who of Argentina's economic elite.
4. **Result:** The Argentine state was left with the debt; the private sector held the dollars offshore.

### Key Connectors

- **Luis Caputo** (current Economy Minister under Milei): Former JP Morgan Argentina head of Bonds/Stocks Trading (1994-1998), later Deutsche Bank. Was Finance Minister under Macri when the 2018 IMF loan was negotiated. Now leads economic policy under Milei and negotiated the 2025 IMF deal.
- **Nicolas Caputo:** Macri's closest friend and business partner. Identified among the top 100 capital flight agents in the BCRA report.
- **Santiago Bausili** (current BCRA President under Milei): Former JP Morgan and Deutsche Bank director, worked with Luis Caputo. Now runs the central bank.

### The Revolving Door

The same individuals who managed Argentina's relationship with international banks and the IMF under Macri now hold the equivalent positions under Milei:
- Luis Caputo: Finance Minister (Macri) → Economy Minister (Milei)
- Santiago Bausili: Finance Secretary (Macri) → BCRA President (Milei)

## 4. Blanqueo as Reverse Capital Flight

### What It Is

The "blanqueo" (asset regularization/tax amnesty) under Milei allows Argentines to declare previously undeclared assets — domestic and offshore — with minimal or zero tax penalty. It is effectively the legalization of capital flight proceeds.

### Results

| Phase | Period | Amount Declared |
|-------|--------|----------------|
| Phase 1 | Ended Nov 8, 2024 | USD $22.5B (of which $20.1B cash) |
| Phase 2 | Ended Feb 7, 2025 | Additional $8.7B |
| Phase 3 | Ended May 7, 2025 | TBD |
| **Total (Phases 1-2)** | | **USD $31.3B** |

- 278,000+ taxpayers entered the program
- 267,076 accounts declared (16,290 abroad)
- 47,815 properties regularized (45,646 domestic, 2,169 abroad)
- Phase 1 results exceeded government expectations of $10B maximum

### The Circular Flow

```
2015-2019: State borrows from IMF → Dollars sold on FX market → Purchased by elite →
Moved offshore as undeclared assets

2024-2025: Same assets "regularized" via blanqueo → No meaningful tax penalty →
Assets now legal without accountability for original capital flight
```

### Key Concern: No Requirement to Repatriate

Unlike previous blanqueos, the current program does not require assets held abroad to be brought back to Argentina. This means offshore wealth can be legalized in place.

### FATF Risk

Argentina narrowly avoided being placed on the FATF "grey list" for money laundering in 2024. A grey listing would have blocked the blanqueo entirely (the legislation prohibits externalization from FATF grey-list countries). The Supreme Court's last-minute commitment to present pending cases was what avoided the listing. Argentina remains under "enhanced follow-up" and must report back within one year.

## 5. Current Debt Exposure (as of Q3 2025)

### Total External Debt

| Category | Amount |
|----------|--------|
| **Total gross external debt** | **USD $316.9B (46.7% of GDP)** |
| Debt to international organizations | USD $96.5B (~30% of total) |
| — of which IMF | ~$58B (~60% of multilateral debt) |
| — of which IDB + World Bank | ~$29B (~30% of multilateral debt) |

Argentina is the **IMF's single largest borrower**, exceeding the combined debt of Ukraine, Egypt, Pakistan, Ecuador, Ivory Coast, Kenya, and Bangladesh.

### New Multilateral Commitments (2025)

| Institution | New Commitment | Details |
|-------------|---------------|---------|
| IMF | USD $20B (48-month EFF) | $12B disbursed immediately (Apr 2025); first review Jul 2025 |
| World Bank | USD $12B support package | Announced Apr 2025 |
| IDB | USD $10B over 3 years | $1.2B approved in initial tranche |
| **Total new multilateral** | **~$42B** | |

### IMF Payment Schedule

The 2025 EFF runs through 2029 with:
- Q2 2025: $12B (disbursed)
- Jun 2025 review: $2B
- Late 2025 review: $1B
- 2026-2029: 7 semi-annual reviews, ~$700M each

### Conditionality

Key conditions attached to the 2025 IMF program:
1. **Zero-overall fiscal deficit** rule enshrined in law by Dec 2026
2. **Revenue-neutral tax reform** proposal by Dec 2025, implementation starting 2026
3. **Pension reform** by Dec 2026
4. **Exchange rate flexibility**: managed float within 1,000-1,400 ARS/USD band (widening 1%/month)
5. **Lifting capital controls** (achieved Apr 2025)
6. **Privatization** of state-owned enterprises (Aerolineas Argentinas and others)

## 6. Structural Adjustment and Privatization Under Milei

### Deregulation Framework

- **DNU 70/23** (Dec 2023): Emergency decree repealing/modifying ~300 laws
- **Ley de Bases** (Jun 2024): Broad economic reform package passed by Congress
- **Ministry of Deregulation** created under Federico Sturzenegger with power to repeal/amend decrees without parliamentary approval

### Key Reforms Aligned with IMF Conditionality

| Reform | Status | IMF Alignment |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Fiscal surplus (1.6% primary) | Met with margin (May 2025) | Core performance criterion |
| Lifting cepo (capital controls) | Completed Apr 2025 | Structural benchmark |
| Privatization of SOEs | In progress (Aerolineas, others) | Structural reform target |
| Labor market deregulation | Partially implemented | Structural benchmark |

| Energy subsidy reduction | Ongoing since 2022 | EFF condition |

### Capital Flight Under Milei (2025)

Early data from the new IMF program shows a repeat pattern: in July 2025, private sector capital flight (FAE) in just three months exceeded by 18.8% the total dollars disbursed by the IMF thus far. An estimated 40%+ of the first $12B disbursement left the country within weeks.

## 7. Summary: The Cycle

```
1. Argentina borrows from IMF/multilaterals
2. Dollars enter BCRA reserves
3. Government sells dollars on FX market to defend peso
4. Concentrated economic elite purchases dollars
5. Dollars leave as capital flight (FAE)
6. Economy destabilizes → new crisis
7. New government negotiates new IMF loan to pay old one
8. Blanqueo legalizes the offshore proceeds
9. Return to step 1
```
